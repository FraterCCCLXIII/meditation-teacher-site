What to Expect

This 7-day silent retreat blends traditional Zen practice with Tibetan nondual teachings and Neo-Platonic wisdom. Through sustained meditation, we'll explore the luminous ground of awareness that is your true nature.

Each day includes:

  • Guided and silent meditation sessions
  • Wisdom teachings from multiple traditions
  • Mindful walking in nature
  • Private interviews with Paul
  • Evening contemplative practices

Who This Is For

This retreat is designed for both new and experienced practitioners ready to deepen their meditation practice. Whether you're seeking clarity, peace, or direct realization of your true nature, this container will support your journey.

You'll benefit most if you:

  • Have some meditation experience (beginner-friendly instruction provided)
  • Are ready to disconnect from daily distractions
  • Feel called to explore consciousness beyond concepts
  • Want to integrate spiritual insight into daily life

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring?

Comfortable clothing for sitting meditation, a reusable water bottle, journal, any meditation props you prefer (we'll have cushions and chairs available), and weather-appropriate layers. A detailed packing list will be sent upon registration.

Is this retreat suitable for beginners?

Yes! While some meditation experience is helpful, Paul provides clear instruction suitable for all levels. The simplicity of silent retreat often makes it more accessible than complex techniques.

What's your cancellation policy?

Full refunds available up to 30 days before the retreat. 50% refund between 30-14 days. No refunds within 14 days, but you may transfer your registration to someone else.

Will there be any talking at all?

Instructional periods and private interviews with Paul will involve speaking, but most of the retreat will be in noble silence. We find this deepens the experience significantly.

What COVID precautions are you taking?

We follow all local health guidelines and will update participants as the retreat approaches. Currently, we recommend but don't require vaccination.
